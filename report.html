Good afternoon Sugarloafers,

What a morning we’ve had here in the mountains of Western Maine! Those who hit the hill first-thing this AM were taking terrific early-season turns under beautiful bluebird skies with plenty of sunshine. We’ve since seen increasing cloud cover overhead since roughly 11:00am, and expect this to remain through last chair today. Snowmaking will be shutting things down on Narrow Gauge around 4:00pm today and continuing work on The Landing, Sluice, and Lower Winter's Way tonight.

Looking ahead to tomorrow, we'll have pockets of sunshine (cue Natasha Bedingfield) with a side of snow showers as temperatures begin a trend of moderation: dress for low 20s at the summit with low 30s by the base.

We expect to have the same 10 trails for tomorrow, spanning across 56 acres and serviced by our 2 scheduled lifts: the SuperQuad will start spinning at 8:30am, with Timberline following not-too-far-behind at 9:00am. Please note that there will be no beginner terrain available from the top of either lift, and uphill traffic is currently limited to the hours of 7:00am to 8:30am.

Last, but not least, we are entering the final hours of our Cyber Sale, meaning it’s your final chance to purchase a Maine 4-Day ticket pack or lock in the lowest rates on New England College and Nitro passes, plus save on lift tickets and winter lodging! Sale ends at midnight tonight.

Signing off to Snow Reporter Chris,
