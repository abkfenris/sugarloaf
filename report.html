Good morning, Sugarloafers.

Wow, how about that moon last night and this morning? Just incredible! Hope everyone got to enjoy it, because the final full moon of 2021 is going to be obscured by a multitude of perfectly symmetrical pieces of fine art falling from the sky.

Our grooming team was hard at work last night, chopping and grinding away at the result of Mother Nature’s temper over the last week. On the positive side, they have been doing it for years and are going to have all 148 acres of available terrain canvased with corduroy for our enjoyment today. The snowmaking crew was quick on the draw and fired up operations on Boardwalk, Skidder, and Whiffletree; they will continue working into today for as long as they can. Eventually, with our incoming storm, the winds are going to switch to an unfavorable direction for Skidder and Boardwalk, which will most likely need to be shut down. Whiffletree, however, has hydrants on the East Side and so they will hopefully be able to continue through the storm.

Today, expect things to feel nippy this morning with temps around 20 degrees, gradually making it to the mid 20’s by afternoon. Winds have calmed down for now but are expected to rise again today only to the 10-30mph range, which will feel like a pleasant breeze compared to yesterday. Snow will begin falling right around last chair today, and will be heaviest between 8:00pm and midnight tonight, so plan for a snowy ramble as you go about your Saturday night delights. By tomorrow morning we are looking to have 5-8 inches of beauty to revel in for the soon-to-be best Sunday shred session of the '21-22 ski season.

Join us this evening for our annual Tree Lighting Festival—and Santa Claus will be coming to town, accompanied by Amos and friends! Come celebrate the season in Sugarloaf style with warm treats, music on the Beach, fireworks, and more.

Catch you here in the afternoon,

Chris

 

COVID-19 Protocols

When it comes to operating safely, we're all in. In accordance with current CDC guidance, face coverings/masks are recommended while indoor for all guests, regardless of vaccination status. At this time, there are no capacity limits in our buildings or on our lift operations. Please take a moment to review our Safety Guidelines before visiting the mountain.

Uphill Access

The mountain opens to uphill travel at 7:00am, and to downhill traffic at 8:30am; no downhill traffic is permitted prior to 8:30am. As always, downhill traffic is limited to open terrain only. All uphill skiers and riders must check the Ski Patrol whiteboard, located on the trail map sign at the bottom of the SuperQuad, every day prior to climbing. 

Uphill Access Routes

Uphill activities are limited at all times to the following trails:

  • Windrow
  • West Mountain
  • Horseshoe
  • Timberline
