Good afternoon Sugarloafers,

Temps remained cool today keeping things fast and sporty for our opening day of the Baxter Outdoors Reggae Fest. The first performance is tonight with a free 21+ show in the King Pine room with Reggae rockers The Equalites, starting at 9pm.

With cold temperatures afoot, the grooming team will be able to hit the hill tonight and groom out a wide variety of terrain for tomorrows Jah riders. Remember if you are feeling a little to irie tomorrow and forget any equipment or use a wax and a fresh edge you can always visit The Downhill Supply Company or visit the easiest of riders at the Sugarloaf Board Room.

The onset of this storm will start to happen anytime now. Tonight we are looking at a wintery mix with temps right around freezing at the base. Friday looks to hold the heaviest rates of precipitation, with temps in the low 30s on the upper mountain and a high temp of around 40 at the base. Winds might become an issue tomorrow with speeds ranging from 15-35mph with gusts to 55mph on the summit. With winds from the Southeast it is hard to anticipate how they will affect lift operations tomorrow. If I were to make an educated guess I would say we’ll at least have the Quad for tomorrow as winds die of into Friday afternoon and night.

Safe travels on the roads of creation,

Chris
