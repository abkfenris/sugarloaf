Good afternoon, Sugarloaf skiers and riders.

Despite not getting over single digit temperatures today, it was a beautiful morning here at the Loaf: the sun and mild winds created a pleasant atmosphere that should remain until the sun sets, and with it, another bluebird day at Sugarloaf in the books.

For those skiing tomorrow, 82 trails and 436 acres will be waiting for you! We should see a warmer and more overcast day with a range of 23-27 degrees byt he base with a span of 13-17 degrees at the summit. There's chance of showers tonight, which could result in an inch of snowfall leading into a mostly cloudy day; winds are expected to be SSE 10-16mph and WSW/SSW 20-35 at the base and top, respectively.

The terrain park crew is excited to have Stomping Grounds back in action after wrapping up the Greg Francouer Bumps & Jumps Event from this weekend; they also have freestyle skiers and riders on their toes as we await the final 2 Sugarloaf terrain parks, Drop Line and Sidewinder. Today’s open parks feature Boardwalk (small), Spurline (medium), Stomping Grounds (medium/large), and Upper Tote (small/medium).

Tonight, our groomers will be out there throwing down lines on their usual list of weekday trails; meanwhile, snowmaking will continue to work on blasting Competition Hill, Drop Line, Timberline Extension, and Upper Gondi Line into a world of white. Looking ahead, cold should reset into the end of the week, with this pattern prevailing through the end of the month; it looks like chance for substantial show comes next weekend.

Brady
